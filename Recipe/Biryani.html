Spicy Mutton Biryani

Description

Mutton Biryani is a royal, fragrant dish made with succulent pieces of marinated mutton layered between long-grain basmati rice and slow-cooked to perfection. It's packed with traditional spices and flavor.

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton (bone-in, cleaned)
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tsp biryani masala or garam masala
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom)
  • Salt and oil/ghee as needed

Steps

  1. Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Boil with salt and whole spices until 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Pressure cook or slow-cook mutton with turmeric, salt, and water until tender. Set aside.
  3. In a pot, fry onions until golden. Remove half for layering later.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste, chopped tomatoes, and spices to the pot. Cook until oil separates.
  5. Add yogurt and the cooked mutton. Simmer until thick and flavorful.
  6. In a large pot, layer rice, mutton masala, chopped herbs, and fried onions.
  7. Seal the pot with a tight lid or dough and cook on low heat (dum) for 25â€“30 minutes.
  8. Fluff gently and serve hot with raita, chutney, or salad.
